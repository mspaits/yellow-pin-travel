Points and Miles Strategy Advisor

If you want to start traveling with points and miles, I can be your strategy advisor. I can teach you the ins and outs of getting started and or advise you on continuing your journey. Points and miles can give you experiences that may otherwise be out of your price range. I can take you from a beginner to an expert by sharing the tips and strategies that work to take your travel further.

Points and miles can open up opportunities to make your money go farther or get travel experiences you may never want to pay the cash price for. They can make a weekend getaway (effectively) free. They can enhance your cash trip with a stay on points at an incredible resort. Points and miles can make your child's request to see a capybara in the wild an easier “yes”. Or they can take a cash pain point away from necessary travel. There is no wrong way to use them once you have them.

Most points and miles are accumulated through credit cards; cards give you the biggest boosts through signup bonuses or every day spend (some multipliers are 5X points on every dollar you spend for a specific category). Signing up for new credit cards generally requires at least good/very good credit to begin with and when cards are opened responsibly, your score does not fluctuate in any meaningful way.

My credit score after opening several recent cards

There are countless cards out there that all offer slightly different reasons for existing. I can help you figure out the right ones for you and your goals. You only open one credit card at a time and space out the new accounts before a second is opened. There is never a bad time to start and if you decide points and miles aren't for you, all it takes is closing those card accounts that you opened that don't serve you anymore (if you want to achieve the sign-up bonus, you complete that spend requirement and then don't need to spend on them again before you close at the year mark; you want to keep a card open for at least a year to keep yourself in good company with the issuing bank).

Some credit cards offer perks or a coupon book that can be used every month, quarter, semi-annual, or year period. Most of these types of cards come with an annual fee; however, it can be very easy to get at least the annual fee (if not more) back from the various perks or coupons (as they're frequently called). This can look like booking a hotel through the credit card portal to get $300 back as statement credit or using a specific credit card for payment that will reimburse TSA Precheck/Global Entry as a statement credit or using a specific card to pay for an order at Five Guys every month to get reimbursed as statement credit or using a specific card to pay for tickets from StubHub for reimbursement as a statement credit (among a wide array of other perks that the various cards offer). So not only do you get value from the points and miles from a signup bonus and purchases, but you can get value from the card itself. I can help you find the path that will work best for your lifestyle and travel needs.
